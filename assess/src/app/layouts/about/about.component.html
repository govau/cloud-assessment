What is the Cloud Assessment Tool (CAT)?

The Cloud Assessment Framework (CAT) helps government agencies determine if a cloud service is suitable for its business needs and operational context.

It asks a series of questions to help you identify requirements around compliance, commercial considerations, operability, support and security.

It also provides information and resources to help you understand these requirements and make informed decisions about which cloud services may be suitable for your needs.

What is it NOT?

The CAT is not a standardised or objective assessment of a cloud vendor or service.

You should not use the output of the assessment as a definitive indicator (yes or no) of whether you should use the assessed service. It is designed as a tool to help you decide if a service is fit for your purposes.

Who is the CAT for?

The CAT is designed for any commonwealth agency staff considering using a cloud-based IT service in their organisation.

How long will it take?

The length of the assessment depends on a few factors, including the nature of the service you are considering and how much of the requested information you have at hand.

The progress bar at the top of each screen indicates how far along you are in the assessment. The number of questions may change depending on your answers.

You can pause the assessment at any time by clicking the Save & Exit button. To resume your assessment, return to https://assess.cloud.gov.au using the same device and browser. You will then be prompted to pick up from where you left off.

For more information on how to prepare for the assessment, read the Prepare page.

What will the assessment tell me?

When you submit your responses, you will be provided with a tailored assessment report. It will highlights any potential misalignment between the cloud service and your needs.

Additional information will be provided to help you understand the results and next steps.

What happens if I need input from my colleagues?

Some of the questions may require you additional information from others within your organisation.

In these cases, you can leave your partially completed questionnaire open in your browser while you contact your colleagues, then return and complete the assessment at a later time.

At this stage, the CAT does not support multiple users completing a single assessment, though this may be considered for future enhancements.

What will the DTA do with the information I provide?

The questionnaire does not ask you to provide any personal or identifiable details.

Information relating to the assessment of the cloud service and vendor will be captured by the DTA to share with other Government Agencies.

In future versions, these assessments may be published on this website. Other information in the questionnaire may be used by the DTA to help improve the CAT.
