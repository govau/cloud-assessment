Preparing for an assessment

The Cloud Assessment Tool (CAT) asks you a series of questions about the cloud service you're assessing and how you expect to use it. The questions are broken into 7 categories.

We’ve provided some suggestions below about information you may wish to review prior before starting the assessment. But you don’t need to have this information before you start the assessment.

Overview

These are top level questions to provide context around the cloud service.

You should know the:

  • name of the cloud service and vendor
  • approximate number of users who will use it
  • primary purpose for the cloud service

Vendor

These questions are about the provider of the cloud service and the reputation and viability of the organisation.

Familiarise yourself with:

  • the vendor's history and experience
  • other agencies that have used the vendor
  • any red flags that suggest the vendor may be unable to provide the service at the level you require

Commercials

These questions look at the contract or Service Level Agreement (SLA) the cloud service provides.

Familiarise yourself with:

  • the terms and conditions provided by the cloud service
  • how the cloud service handles content ownership and data privacy
  • how the vendor deals with exit provisions and additional usage pricing

You may also want to talk to your Legal and Finance teams to determine your agency's requirements, so you can ensure the cloud service can meet them.

Compliance

These questions look at how you can assure you are meeting the appropriate compliance requirements when using the cloud service.

Familiarise yourself with:

  • your obligations relating to the Privacy Act 1988
  • your obligations relating to the Archives Act 1983
  • your obligations relating to the Freedom of Information Act 1982
  • ASD Certification and the Certified Cloud Services list
  • the Information Security Registered Assessors Program (IRAP)
  • the Information Government Security Manual
  • WCAG compliance under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992.
  • the location of the cloud services’ data centres who has access to the data

Security

These questions look at the security aspects of the cloud service.

Familiarise yourself with:

  • the security of data both in transit and at rest
  • how the service manages and tracks access
  • the physical security of the data centres

Operability

These questions look at the performance of the cloud service in terms of scalability, connectivity and general use.

Familiarise yourself with:

  • any past performance data the vendor makes available
  • how the service manages availability, compatibility and load balancing
  • how the service manages data transfer and portability
  • any information that speaks to the ease of use of the product

Support

These questions look at the resources and support provided by the vendor to help you use the cloud service.

Familiarise yourself with the availability of:

  • any online training resources – such as videos or tutorials
  • customer support and community-based help
  • technical experts to assist with implementation and maintenance
